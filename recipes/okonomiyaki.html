Okonomiyaki Recipe

Description

Okonomiyaki is a savory Japanese pancake made with a batter of flour, eggs, and shredded cabbage, then topped with a variety of ingredients such as pork, seafood, or vegetables. The name comes from the words “okonomi” (meaning “as you like”) and “yaki” (meaning “grill” or “cook”), reflecting its customizable nature. It’s commonly cooked on a griddle, drizzled with a sweet and tangy okonomiyaki sauce, Japanese mayonnaise, and finished with aonori (seaweed flakes) and katsuobushi (bonito flakes).

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2/3 cup water or dashi stock
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 cups finely shredded cabbage
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • 4 to 6 strips of thinly sliced pork belly (or bacon)
  • Okonomiyaki sauce
  • Japanese mayonnaise
  • Aonori (seaweed flakes)
  • Katsuobushi (bonito flakes)

Steps

  1. In a large bowl, mix together the flour, water (or dashi), and eggs until smooth.
  2. Add the shredded cabbage and chopped green onions, stirring until everything is well coated in the batter.
  3. Heat a lightly oiled skillet or griddle over medium heat.
  4. Pour a portion of the batter onto the skillet and shape it into a round pancake about 6 inches wide.
  5. Lay slices of pork belly (or bacon) on top of the pancake.
  6. Cook for 4 to 5 minutes, until the bottom is golden brown. Flip carefully and cook another 4 to 5 minutes, pressing gently with a spatula.
  7. When both sides are cooked through and crispy, transfer to a plate.
  8. Drizzle generously with okonomiyaki sauce and Japanese mayonnaise.
  9. Sprinkle with aonori and katsuobushi before serving hot.
