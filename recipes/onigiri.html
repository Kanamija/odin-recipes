Miso-Butter Yaki Onigiri Recipe

Description

Yaki onigiri are Japanese rice balls grilled until the outside turns golden and crisp. This version gets a glossy miso–butter glaze (with a touch of soy) for a umami-rich, restaurant-worthy finish. Serve as a snack, side, or party bite—bonus points for a sprinkle of furikake and a shiso leaf wrap.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups cooked Japanese short-grain rice (warm, not hot)
  • Salt (for seasoning your hands)
  • Neutral oil (for the pan or grill)
  • Glaze:
    • 1 tbsp unsalted butter, melted
    • 1 tbsp white or red miso
    • 1 tsp soy sauce
    • 1 tsp mirin (optional)
    • 1 tsp sugar or honey (optional, helps caramelize)
  • Fancy toppers (optional): furikake, toasted sesame seeds, finely sliced green onion, shiso leaves, nori strips

Instructions

  1. Make the glaze: In a small bowl, stir together melted butter, miso, soy, and (if using) mirin and sugar until smooth.
  2. Shape the rice: Wet your hands with water, sprinkle with a little salt, and scoop ~1/2 cup rice. Gently press into a firm triangle or round. Repeat to make 6–8 onigiri.
  3. Pan-grill: Heat a nonstick skillet or well-oiled cast-iron over medium. Add a film of oil. Place onigiri and cook 3–4 minutes per side until lightly golden and set. (You can also use a grill—oil the grates well.)
  4. Glaze & crisp: Reduce heat slightly. Brush tops with miso-butter, flip, and brush the other side. Cook 1–2 minutes per side more, flipping once or twice, until glossy and caramelized. Don’t rush—let the surface crisp.
  5. Finish: Sprinkle with furikake or sesame, add green onion, and wrap a strip of nori or a shiso leaf around the bottom if you like. Serve hot.
